Gilding the Lily

  • by Jeff Caulfield
  • under “publishing”

I tried a variety of static site generators on my way to rebuilding my personal website: hexo, hugo and sculpin, just to name a few. These all seem very complicated for what they purport to be, and definitely overkill for my needs.

Eventually, I stumbled across embellish. It’s written in Python. It’s very lightweight. It doesn’t require a ton of files to get started. I was able to get something off the ground quickly without becoming bogged down in excessive detail. Very nice.

That said, I feel that there’s some “fat” that could yet be trimmed. I don’t understand the need to use Haml for templates when Jinja is fine on its own. I’m not using compiled assets (CoffeeScript, Sass and so forth), although that’s not to say that I might not want to do so later on. I’d like the command-line to auto-detect “site.yaml” when it is present. I’d also like to remove or at least rename the “site.cache” file.

At the same time, there are a few small but important features that are missing. A sitemap and a syndication feed in Atom and/or RSS format should be a given. A “draft” mode for content in progress also seems essential. Pagination would be useful for large archive indexes. Finally, I’d like a simple way to add site-specific extensions (Python scripts) to customise the render process, if at all possible.

This all seems acheivable. I think I’ll call it gild as in “gilding the lily” in keeping with the theme of embellishment.

What could possibly go wrong?
